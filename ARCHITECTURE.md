# Architecture Documentation

## Core Philosophy

### The "Shell Game" Headline
> "The greatest trick allocation ever pulled was convincing everyone it was observation."

This headline refers to the epistemic failure of treating modeled geographic proxies as direct measurements. By quantifying "structured uncertainty," we transition from merely auditing allocation to enabling a user-defined context where the researcher acknowledges the transition from observation to modeled estimation.

## Technical Components

### 1. Audit Engine (geoDeltaAudit)
The core logic for quantifying perturbation ($\Delta X$) across geographic transformations. It treats crosswalks as directional allocations rather than invertible transformations.

### 2. Conceptual Framework (shellgame)
A standardized protocol for documenting the assumptions made during geographic data integration, specifically focusing on membership definitions and crosswalk selection.

### 3. Visualization Layer
Interactive components designed to make the "invisible" perturbation visible to the analyst, using high-contrast "Lab Report" aesthetics to emphasize the technical nature of the audit.
